Diabetic Retinopathy

Quick Info

  • When the blood vessels that nourish the retina get cut off unable to get blood the retina
  • May cause blurred vision, dark / empty spots and squiggly lines in your vision
  • Hard to spot in the early stages but if not caught in time may lead to permanent vision loss
  • Usually affects people who have type 1 or 2 diabetes
  • Can be treated using lasers or freezing very quickly

What is Diabetic Retinopathy

Diabetic retinopathy is when the amount of sugar in the blood is too much that the blood vessels that nourish the retina get clogged up

when this happens, the eye tries to make more blood vessels but can't make ones that properly function

Notice darks spots in your vision

Vision gets blurrier

IF not caught in time, may lead to vision loss

Causes of Diabetic Retinopathy

Diabetic Retinopathy usually happens when you have either Type 1 or Type 2 diabetes

How to treat it

There are 2 surgeries you can do to treat it

You can either use freezing to fix / mend the retina and then use a air bubble to keep the retina in place so it heals properly

The other treatment is to use lasers to help heal the abnormal blood vessels
