Color Blindness

Quick Info

  • When the colors you see are duller / brighter than what the intended color is
  • Hard time differentiating reds, blues, greens, or yellows
  • Usually people who are color blind, usually inherit it
  • May also be caused by age, alcoholism, chemicals or diseases like Retinal Detatchment
  • No way to treat color blindness
  • Gllasses or contact lenses that you can wear to help negate the effects of color blindness

What is Color blindness

Color blindness is when colors you look at seem different than what they are intended to seem as

They might look more dull, more bright, etc.

It also causes a hard time to differentiate similar shades ofd the same color

Happens when the color receptors in your retina arent working properly causing the optical nerve to send the wrong singal to the brain

Causes of color blindness

Most common cause of color blindness is inheriting it

It can also be caused by aging, alcoholism, chemicals, and certain eye diseases

How to treat it

There is no way to treat color blindness

There are however glasses / contact lenses you can wear that helps negate the effects of color blindness
